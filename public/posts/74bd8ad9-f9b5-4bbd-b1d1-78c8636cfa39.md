Hello and welcome to my blog!

Throughout my career, I've found myself at the intersection of scale, reliability, and innovation—especially when it comes to monitoring mission-critical systems.

At Prudential, I was entrusted with a legacy network monitoring tool that had grown outdated, yet was responsible for the health of over 400,000 monitors across the enterprise. As the technical owner, I not only maintained the system but also became a key advisor to executives, guiding product decisions as we planned its replacement. This experience taught me the high stakes of architectural change: when you're responsible for coverage at this scale, every decision must balance innovation with the guarantee that nothing slips through the cracks.

Later, at Amazon, I faced a similar challenge on a new frontier. I led a team of engineers to design a system that could automatically detect new infrastructure as it was created—deploying monitors that scaled up and down in real time with hosts and VIPs across the organization. Here, I learned the power of standardization: by building automation into the very fabric of our monitoring, we enabled the business to move faster, with confidence that reliability would scale alongside growth.

Of course, not every deployment goes perfectly. I'll never forget the day we accidentally deployed monitors across a significant portion of the enterpise's  infrastructure and started alerting a single queue for every one of them. For about ten minutes, a support team's queue lit up like a Christmas tree—until we quickly pointed the queue at ourselves and rolled back the change. It was a humbling reminder: at scale, even a small misconfiguration can make a very big noise.

These experiences have shaped my philosophy: true technical leadership means not just solving today's problems, but building systems that empower others to innovate safely at scale. And sometimes, it means learning to laugh, fix fast, and share the story.

If you've ever had a monitoring mishap, or if you're passionate about building for scale, I'd love to hear your stories. Here's to learning, growing, and building together!

— Eric Caskey
